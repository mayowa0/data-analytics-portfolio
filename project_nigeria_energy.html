Background
Why Energy Access?

Nigeria is Africa's largest economy yet ranks among the world's worst for electricity reliability. With an installed capacity of ~12,500 MW but actual generation averaging 3,500–4,000 MW, the gap between potential and delivery is enormous.

This project uses publicly available data from the World Bank, Nigeria Electricity Regulatory Commission (NERC), and the Nigerian Bureau of Statistics to map, visualize, and analyse electricity access patterns across all 36 states and 6 geopolitical zones.

Electricity Access by Geopolitical Zone

Access rates vary dramatically across Nigeria's six zones, driven by infrastructure investment, urbanization, and proximity to generation assets.
