Four Analyses
One Cohesive Story

Each section answers a distinct business question using a different statistical method — but all four converge on the same insight: income stability and credit tier are the primary levers in a digital lending business.

Section 01
A/B Hypothesis Testing
Did the new loan application UI significantly improve conversion?
Two-proportion z-test (one-tailed) comparing conversion rates between 3,200 control users (old UI) and 3,150 treatment users (new simplified bank statement upload). Validated with Mann-Whitney U for robustness. Effect size via Cohen's h. Statistical power calculated.
p < 0.001 · +3.6pp lift · Cohen's h = 0.09 · Power = 84%
Section 02
Multiple Linear Regression
What borrower features best predict loan repayment amount?
OLS regression on log-transformed repayment amount. Full assumptions testing: Shapiro-Wilk normality, Levene homoscedasticity, VIF multicollinearity check, Q-Q plot and residual diagnostics. Six predictors including income, credit score, tenure, and employment type.
Adj. R² = 0.72 · F-stat significant · All VIF < 5
Section 03
Time Series Forecasting
Can we forecast next quarter's disbursements from historical trends?
18 months of weekly disbursement data. ADF stationarity test → seasonal decomposition → Holt-Winters exponential smoothing model. Bootstrap-simulated 80% and 95% prediction intervals. Validated on held-out 13-week test set.
MAPE < 5% on validation · Q2 forecast: ₦2.8B ±10%
Section 04
Chi-Square + One-Way ANOVA
Are default rates significantly different across borrower tiers?
Chi-square test of independence (default status × loan tier) with Cramér's V effect size. One-way ANOVA on repayment amounts across income segments, with Levene test for equal variances and Bonferroni-corrected post-hoc pairwise comparisons.
χ² significant · η² = 0.41 (large effect) · All post-hoc pairs significant
