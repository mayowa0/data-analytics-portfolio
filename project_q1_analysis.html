What This Analysis Covers

This 29-slide deck was prepared as the Q1 2026 business performance review for a Nigerian B2C digital lending platform. The analysis spans five core areas: executive summary, disbursement analysis, repayment and collections, conversion and process efficiency, and strategic recommendations for Q2.

Data was pulled from the internal loan management system, credit bureau feeds, and product analytics platform, then transformed and visualized to tell a clear, decision-ready story for leadership. All figures shown here are dummy data — the structure and methodology are real.

Recommendations
4 Strategic Actions for Q2
Earn More
Scale the Salaried Segment
Salaried borrowers deliver the best risk-adjusted volume — low default rates combined with higher ticket sizes. Set Q2 acquisition targets specifically for Score Band A–B salaried applicants.
→ Brief sales and credit teams on new segment targets by Q2 kickoff
Reduce Wastage
Tighten Score Band D Criteria
Score Band D self-employed borrowers represent a disproportionate share of defaults relative to their volume contribution. Restrict this combination on tenures under 60 days.
→ Update credit policy; review in underwriting team meeting
Product Decisions
Introduce 45-Day Tenure Option
+ Pilot Partial Repayment for High-Ticket Borrowers
45-day tenures could reduce defaults by reducing end-of-term pressure. Allow borrowers to pay a portion mid-tenure on loans over ₦200k.
→ Product team to scope for Q2 2026 roadmap
Retention & Growth
Build an Automated Upgrade Engine
Only a small fraction of repeat borrowers upgrade to a higher loan band. A structured upgrade trigger after the 2nd repayment — plus a retention campaign targeting first-loan-only borrowers — could unlock significant Q2 incremental volume.
→ CRM team: design campaign targeting 1st-loan borrowers within 90 days of repayment
